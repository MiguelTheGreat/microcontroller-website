Texas Instruments

Texas Instruments (TI) is an American multinational semiconductor company headquartered in Dallas, Texas. Founded in 1930, it specializes in designing, manufacturing, and selling analog and embedded processing chips. TI is one of the top 10 semiconductor companies worldwide, focusing on developing analog chips and embedded processors, which account for over 80% of its revenue. The company operates globally, with a commitment to manufacturing excellence and technological innovation, serving diverse markets including industrial, automotive, and personal electronics.

  • Hercules Safety Microcontrollers are ARM-based MCUs (Cortex-R4/R5 and Cortex-M3/M4 cores) engineered specifically for functional safety-critical systems. They include hardware safety features like dual-core lockstep operation, ECC-protected memories, built-in self-test (BIST), and error signaling to meet certifications such as ISO 26262 (automotive) and IEC 61508 (industrial).
